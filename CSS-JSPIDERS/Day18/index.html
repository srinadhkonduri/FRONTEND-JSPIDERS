Hyderabad

The Charminar is famous as a symbol of Hyderabad, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and an iconic monument showcasing Indo-Islamic architecture. It is also a popular tourist destination and a site for religious and cultural celebration. In 1591, the ruler of the Qutub Shahi dynasty, Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutub Shahi, got this masterpiece constructed after visiting Hyderabad from Golconda. The four minarets of Charminar represent the four directions — North, East, South, and West — signifying glory and protection from all sides of the world.
